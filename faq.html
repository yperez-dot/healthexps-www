About Medicare & Health Insurance.

Find answers to the most common questions about Medicare enrollment, costs, plan types, and coverage in South Florida.

📅Medicare Enrollment
Most people enroll when they turn 65. Your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) is a 7-month window: 3 months before your 65th birthday month, the month you turn 65, and 3 months after. If you miss this window, you may face late enrollment penalties and have to wait for the General Enrollment Period (January 1 – March 31). See all enrollment periods →
If you're already receiving Social Security benefits, you'll be automatically enrolled in Parts A and B starting the month you turn 65. If you're not receiving Social Security, you need to actively enroll at ssa.gov or your local Social Security office during your Initial Enrollment Period. Don't assume it happens automatically.
Yes. If you or your spouse are still working and covered by an employer health plan based on active employment, you can delay Part B without penalty. When you eventually lose that employer coverage, you'll get a Special Enrollment Period (SEP) of 8 months to enroll. Important: COBRA and retiree coverage do NOT qualify for this exception.
Missing your Initial Enrollment Period without qualifying for an exception can result in late enrollment penalties. Part B adds 10% to your premium for each 12-month period you were eligible but didn't enroll — and this penalty is permanent. Part D adds 1% per month late. Enrolling on time is critical. Learn about penalties →
You can switch during the Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) — October 15 to December 7 each year. Changes take effect January 1. You can also switch during the Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (OEP) — January 1 to March 31 — if you're already on an Advantage plan. Some qualifying life events also trigger Special Enrollment Periods.
🏥Medicare Plan Types
Original Medicare (Parts A and B) is the federal program covering hospital and medical services. You can see any doctor nationwide that accepts Medicare, but there's no annual out-of-pocket cap. Medicare Advantage (Part C) is an alternative offered by private insurers — it bundles A and B coverage with extra benefits like dental and vision, and has an annual OOP maximum. However, it restricts you to a provider network. Learn more →
Medigap is private insurance that works alongside Original Medicare to cover the costs Medicare doesn't pay — like the 20% coinsurance, deductibles, and copays. With Plan G (the most popular), you pay only the Part B deductible ($257 in 2026) and Medicare + Medigap cover the rest. You can see any doctor that accepts Medicare — no networks. Compare plan letters →
No. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) only works with Original Medicare. If you enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan, you cannot use a Medigap policy alongside it. You need to choose one path: Original Medicare + Medigap, or Medicare Advantage.
Part D is Medicare's prescription drug coverage. If you're on Original Medicare or a Medigap plan, you need a standalone Part D plan to cover your prescriptions. Most Medicare Advantage plans include drug coverage built in (called MAPD). If you don't enroll in Part D when first eligible and don't have other creditable drug coverage, you'll face a late enrollment penalty.
A Special Needs Plan (SNP) is a type of Medicare Advantage plan tailored to people with specific needs. D-SNPs are for people who qualify for both Medicare and Medicaid (dual-eligible). C-SNPs are for people with certain chronic conditions. D-SNPs in South Florida often have $0 copays and comprehensive care coordination benefits.
💰Medicare Costs & IRMAA
The standard Part B premium is $202.90/month in 2026. Part A is free for most people. Medicare Advantage plans in South Florida often have $0 additional premium (you still pay Part B). If you add a Medigap plan, premiums range from $80–$220+/month in Florida. Higher-income individuals pay more due to IRMAA surcharges. See full 2026 cost breakdown →
IRMAA (Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount) is a surcharge added to your Part B and Part D premiums if your income exceeds certain thresholds. In 2026, it applies to individuals earning above $106,000 and couples above $212,000. Social Security uses your tax return from 2 years prior to calculate it. If your income has dropped recently, you can file a Life-Changing Event appeal. See 2026 IRMAA brackets →
Yes. If your income has dropped due to a qualifying life event — retirement, divorce, death of a spouse, or loss of income-producing property — you can file a Life-Changing Event appeal using Form SSA-44 with Social Security. If approved, your IRMAA will be recalculated using your current income. Call us and we'll point you in the right direction.
The Medicare Savings Program (MSP) is a state-run program that helps people with limited income pay for Medicare costs. In Florida, QMB can pay your Part B premium, deductibles, and copays. SLMB and QI pay your Part B premium. If you qualify for any MSP program, you also automatically qualify for Extra Help with Part D. Check eligibility →

🛡️Coverage Questions
With Original Medicare or a Medigap plan, you do not need a referral to see a specialist — as long as the provider accepts Medicare. With a Medicare Advantage HMO, you typically need a referral from your primary care doctor. PPO plans generally don't require referrals but may charge more for out-of-network care.
Original Medicare does not cover routine dental, vision, or hearing. Medicare Advantage plans often include these benefits — many in South Florida have dental cleanings, eye exams, glasses allowances, and hearing aid benefits included. If you're on Original Medicare, you can add standalone dental and vision plans. Learn about dental & vision coverage →
Yes. People who qualify for both are called dual-eligible beneficiaries. D-SNP plans are designed specifically for dual-eligible individuals and often provide $0 copays and coordinated care. Florida's Medicaid program may also help pay your Medicare premiums and cost-sharing through the Medicare Savings Program.
With Original Medicare or a Medigap plan, you can see any doctor nationwide that accepts Medicare — travel is no issue. With Medicare Advantage, you're covered for emergency and urgent care anywhere in the U.S., but routine care is typically restricted to your plan's service area. If you travel frequently, a PPO or Original Medicare + Medigap is often a better fit.
Original Medicare does not include prescription drug coverage. You need to add a standalone Part D plan, or enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan that includes drug coverage (MAPD). The 2026 out-of-pocket cap for Part D is $2,000 — the first in Medicare's history. If your income is limited, Extra Help can reduce or eliminate your drug costs.
📋ACA & Private Health Insurance
The ACA Marketplace (also called the Health Insurance Exchange) is where you can shop for individual and family health insurance plans that meet the Affordable Care Act's standards. All plans cover 10 essential benefits. Income-based subsidies (Premium Tax Credits) can significantly reduce — or even eliminate — your monthly premium. See ACA options in Miami →
Yes. Premium Tax Credits are available to individuals and families earning between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level — and in many cases above. In 2026, a single person earning up to approximately $60,000 may qualify. Many people pay $0 or close to it after credits are applied. We check your eligibility for free.
COBRA is almost always more expensive than comparable ACA coverage, especially if you qualify for a subsidy. When you lose employer coverage, you have 60 days to enroll in an ACA plan. We usually find better coverage for significantly less than what COBRA costs. Always compare before committing to COBRA. Compare your options →
Open Enrollment runs November 1 – January 15 each year. Outside of that, you can enroll if you experience a qualifying life event — losing job coverage, getting married, having a baby, moving to a new state, or other changes. These trigger a Special Enrollment Period of 60 days.
