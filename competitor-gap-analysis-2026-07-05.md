# Competitor Gap Analysis — healthexps.com vs. Miami Medicare SERPs

## The competitive landscape in one page

**English "money" SERPs are owned by national affiliates, not local brokers.** For "best Medicare Advantage plans Miami 2026," page one is medicare.org (Allstate-owned), medicareadvantage.com, Connie Health, U.S. News, NerdWallet, and SelectQuote. Not a single independent Miami brokerage ranks. These sites win with *data*: plan counts (46 MA plans in Miami-Dade, 40 at $0 premium), enrollment leaders (UHC MedicareMax, Humana Gold Plus), average premiums, star-rating mixes. THEI can replicate this with public CMS data plus something none of them have: local, bilingual, in-person expertise and real E-E-A-T (named licensed agents).

**Spanish SERPs are owned by local Latino agencies — but they're ACA-heavy and Medicare-thin.** "Seguros médicos en Miami" is dominated by Braojos Insurance, TuSeguroMedico, Dulcinea, Quijote, Amor Insurance, and Entérate — long-form Spanish content machines, but almost all focused on Obamacare/private plans. Spanish *Medicare* content is notably weaker (thin doorway-style pages). SFIB (Miami/Doral) is the closest direct competitor: a bilingual Medicare broker with a service-area page targeting Hialeah, Doral, Kendall, etc. The Spanish Medicare lane is winnable, and it's exactly THEI's home turf.

**Nobody owns the clinic/hospital-network queries.** Searches around Leon Medical Centers, CanoHealth-style clinics, Baptist Health, and Jackson networks return only carrier sites and hospital press releases — no broker explains "which plans work with which Miami health system." Given that Miami's Medicare culture revolves around clinic affiliation (Leon alone serves ~40,000+ members), this is the most defensible content moat on this list.

**Vocabulary gap:** Miami's Spanish-speaking market searches "aseguranza," "Obamacare" (not "ACA"), and "cuánto cuesta." THEI's ES pages currently mirror English terminology. Competitors like Braojos write the way people actually search.
