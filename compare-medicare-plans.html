Advantage vs. Supplement vs. Original.

Not sure which path to take? Here's an honest, side-by-side breakdown of every Medicare option — so you can make the right decision for your doctors, budget, and lifestyle.

The 3 paths to Medicare coverage

Every Medicare beneficiary chooses one of three approaches. None is universally better — the right choice depends entirely on your situation.

Original Medicare

The federal baseline — Parts A and B. Covers hospital and medical care. You can see any doctor nationwide that accepts Medicare. No network, no annual OOP cap. Most people add either Medigap or Part D on top.

Part B: $202.90/mo · No OOP cap
Learn about Original Medicare →
Medicare Advantage

A private plan alternative that bundles Parts A + B into one plan. Usually includes dental, vision, hearing, and drug coverage. Annual OOP cap protects you. Network-based — verify your doctors first.

Often $0 premium in South FL · OOP cap required
Learn about Medicare Advantage →
Original Medicare + Medigap

Add a Medigap supplement plan on top of Original Medicare to fill the 20% gap. See any doctor nationwide. Predictable costs — after Plan G, nearly everything is covered. Add a separate Part D for drugs.

Plan G: ~$300–$375/mo · Nationwide access

Who each option is best for

A quick guide to match your situation to the right Medicare path.

Original Medicare is best if...
You plan to add a Medigap plan for full coverage
You want maximum flexibility in choosing any plan type
You're still deciding between Advantage and Medigap
Medicare Advantage is best if...
Your doctors are in-network for available plans
You want dental, vision, and hearing included
A $0 or low monthly premium is important
You want an OOP cap to protect against large bills
You take prescriptions and want one plan for everything
Medigap is best if...
You see specialists who aren't in Advantage networks
You want zero billing surprises — completely predictable costs
You travel frequently inside the United States
You have complex or frequent healthcare needs
You're willing to pay a higher monthly premium for peace of mind
