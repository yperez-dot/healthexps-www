What COBRA actually costs in Florida

COBRA requires you to pay 100% of your employer's premium plus a 2% admin fee. For most Florida residents, that means:

Single person
$800–$1,020
per month
Family coverage
$2,000–$2,800
per month

⚠️ Figures above are estimates based on average Florida market rates. Actual costs vary based on your age, location, income, and health history. A licensed broker will run your personalized quote at no cost — no obligation to enroll.
⚠️ Are you 65 or older and on COBRA?

COBRA is NOT considered creditable coverage for Medicare Part B. Every month you stay on COBRA instead of enrolling in Medicare Part B, you may be accumulating a permanent late enrollment penalty. If you're 65 or older, talk to a licensed broker before your 60-day window closes.

Frequently asked questions

How long do I have to find a COBRA alternative in Florida?
You have 60 days from losing coverage to enroll in an ACA Marketplace plan through a Special Enrollment Period — whether you're in Miami, Orlando, Tampa, or anywhere in Florida.
I'm over 65 and on COBRA — is that okay?
Not always. COBRA is not considered creditable coverage for Medicare Part B. If you're 65 or older staying on COBRA instead of enrolling in Medicare, you may be accumulating a permanent late enrollment penalty. Talk to a licensed broker immediately.
Is COBRA ever worth keeping?
Yes — if you're in active cancer treatment, pregnancy, or need to keep a very specific specialist. Otherwise alternatives are almost always cheaper.
How much does it cost to work with The Health Experts?
