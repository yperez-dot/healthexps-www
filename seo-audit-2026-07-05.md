## 🔴 P0 — Critical (fix before anything else)

### 1. Legacy Wix URLs are still live and cannibalizing the new pages
These old pages are indexed, ranking, and splitting authority with your new pages. Google is currently choosing between duplicates:

| Legacy URL (still live) | Competes with | Action |
|---|---|---|
| `/medicare-insurance-agents` | `/medicare-agent-miami` | 301 → `/medicare-agent-miami` |
| `/medicare-insurance-agents-miami` | `/medicare-agent-miami` | 301 → `/medicare-agent-miami` |
| `/insurance/medicareplans` | `/medicare-plans-miami` | 301 → `/medicare-plans-miami` |
| `/healthcare-insurance-for-seniors` | `/medicare-plans-miami` (serving **identical content**) | 301 → `/medicare-plans-miami` |
| `/medical-insurance-broker` | `/medicare-insurance-agents` content (homepage-style) | 301 → `/` or `/medicare-agent-miami` |
| `/service-page/medicare-made-easy` | Nothing — it's a 2020 webinar page ("our new normal," Silver Sneakers guest) still indexed | 301 → `/resources` or 410 |

**Why P0:** Three separate "Medicare agents Miami" pages are competing against each other for your single most valuable local keyword. Consolidating these will likely do more for rankings than any copy change on this list. Crawl the full old Wix sitemap for more of these — there are almost certainly others.

### 2. EN→ES language toggle points to the wrong page sitewide
On the homepage, Medicare Advantage, Supplement, ACA, FAQ, and Contact pages, the "ES" toggle all point to `/es/planes-de-medicare-miami` — the Spanish Medicare page — regardless of which page you're on. Only the COBRA page does it right (→ `/es/alternativas-cobra-miami`).

- Fix each EN page's ES toggle to its true Spanish equivalent (and `/` → `/es/`).
- Add reciprocal `hreflang="en"` / `hreflang="es"` / `x-default` link tags on every EN/ES pair. Without correct pairing, Google can't associate the versions, and Spanish-query rankings suffer — which is your differentiator.
- This also matches your known open fix: homepage ES link should go to `/es/`, sitewide.

### 3. Contradictory 2026 Medicare cost figures across pages (E-E-A-T + compliance risk)
| Figure | Where it appears | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Part B premium "$185/month in 2026" | medicare-advantage-miami FAQ (×2, incl. give-back "up to the full $185") | $185 was **2025**. 2026 is **$202.90** — which your own FAQ page states correctly. Same site, two answers. |
| Part B deductible "$257 in 2026" | FAQ page + supplement page | $257 was 2025. 2026 Part B deductible is **$283**. |
| Part A deductible "$1,676" | supplement page (Plan G card) | 2025 figure. 2026 is **$1,736**. |
| Part D cap "$2,000 in 2026 — first in history" | FAQ page | $2,000 was 2025; 2026 is indexed to **~$2,100**, and it wasn't new in 2026. |
| MOOP "$9,350 in-network cap" | medicare-advantage-miami hero stat | That's the 2025 limit; CMS lowered the 2026 in-network MOOP to **$9,250**. Verify against the 2026 rate announcement. |
| Plan G premium "~$300–$375/mo" vs "$80+ starting" vs FAQ "Medigap $80–$220+/mo" | supplement page + FAQ | Three conflicting ranges on the same site. Pick real quoted ranges and use them everywhere. |

Wrong cost figures on YMYL pages are exactly what Google's quality systems punish — and what a CMS/TPMO reviewer would flag. Do a single-session sweep: grep the repo for `185`, `257`, `1,676`, `2,000`, `9,350`.

### 4. COBRA page is orphaned
`/cobra-alternatives-miami` is not in the nav or footer of any page — it's only reachable from two in-body FAQ links and the private-insurance page. Orphaned pages get crawled less and rank worse.
- Add "COBRA Alternatives" to the footer "More" column sitewide.
- Add it to the Insurance dropdown (it converts year-round — your highest-intent SEP traffic).

### 5. Inconsistent navigation across pages (multiple template versions live)
- COBRA page nav has **"Annual Enrollment (AEP)" → `/medicare-annual-enrollment-2027`** — no other page has this item. (Great page to have! Put it in the nav sitewide before October.)
- medicare-advantage-miami nav is **missing "Blog"**; other pages have it.

## 🟡 P2–P3 — Content gaps vs. what Miami Medicare searchers want

1. **No standalone Part D page.** Homepage and hub mention Part D, but there's no `/medicare-part-d-miami`. "Part D plans Florida," "best Part D plan for my medications" — you compare formularies as a core service and have no page saying so.
2. **No "Turning 65 in Miami" page.** `/medicare/new-to-medicare` is generic. A localized turning-65 page (IEP timeline widget you already built + Miami-specific carrier landscape + Spanish version) targets the single highest-intent evergreen query. 
3. **No hospital/network content.** Nobody local owns "Medicare Advantage plans that cover Baptist Health," "…Jackson Memorial," "…University of Miami Health," "…Cleveland Clinic Florida," "doctors that accept Medicare in Hialeah/Kendall/Doral." Your whole pitch is "we verify your doctors" — a 4–6 page network hub would be unique, local, and nearly uncontested. (Keep it factual and dated; networks change annually.)
4. **No neighborhood pages.** Hialeah, Kendall, Westchester, Little Havana, Homestead — massive senior + Spanish-speaking populations. Even 3–4 pages ("Medicare plans Hialeah") with genuinely local content (nearby carriers' popular clinics — CanoHealth-style clinic culture, local pharmacies) would convert. Avoid thin doorway pages: each needs real local substance.
5. **AEP page exists (`/medicare-annual-enrollment-2027`) but is hidden** in one page's nav. Surface it sitewide by September 1.
6. **Cost-transparency content:** "How much does Medicare cost in Miami 2026" as its own page (you have the guide; a localized version with real Plan G quotes by age band would earn featured snippets).
7. **Spanish parity check:** confirm every page in this audit has a live `/es/` twin; the ES toggle bug suggests some don't. Spanish "Medicare Miami" queries are lower-competition than English and you're natively positioned to win them.
8. **Homepage puts the phone number ahead of proof:** consider adding license number(s) and NPN near the footer disclaimer — trust marker for YMYL raters and for seniors' adult children doing the vetting.
