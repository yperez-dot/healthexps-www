Your private health insurance options in Florida

Not everyone fits into the same box. Here's how to find what's right for your situation, budget, and timing.

ACA Marketplace Plans

Comprehensive, ACA-compliant coverage with 10 essential benefits. Income-based subsidies can dramatically reduce or eliminate your premium. The best option for most individuals and families.

  • Pre-existing conditions always covered
  • Subsidies available — many pay $0–$50/mo
  • Open Enrollment: Nov 1 – Jan 15
  • Special Enrollment if you lost other coverage
Best for: most individuals and families
See ACA plans →
Short-Term Health Plans

Temporary coverage for gaps between jobs, waiting for Open Enrollment, or other short-term needs. Lower premiums but limited benefits — not ACA-compliant.

  • Coverage in as little as 24 hours
  • Lower monthly premium than ACA
  • Does NOT cover pre-existing conditions
  • Available outside Open Enrollment
Best for: healthy people needing bridge coverage
Group Health for Small Business

Employer-sponsored group coverage for businesses with 2–50 employees. Premium contributions are tax-deductible and help attract and retain talent.

  • Employees get guaranteed coverage
  • Business deducts premium contributions
  • Available year-round — no Open Enrollment
  • We set it up and manage renewals
Best for: small business owners (2–50 employees)
COBRA Alternatives

COBRA lets you keep your former employer plan — but you pay the full premium. That can be $800–$1,800+/month for a family. We almost always find better coverage for less.

  • You have 60 days from job loss to enroll in ACA
  • ACA subsidies often make it far cheaper
  • Same or better coverage, lower cost
  • Don't pay COBRA without comparing first
Most people save $200–$800/month vs. COBRA
