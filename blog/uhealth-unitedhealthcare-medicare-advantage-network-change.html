UHealth Leaving UnitedHealthcare Medicare? Your Miami Options for 2026

If you have a UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage plan and see doctors through the University of Miami Health System (UHealth) — including specialists at Bascom Palmer Eye Institute or Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center — there’s an important network update as of this month, and it may already affect you.

What’s Actually Happening Between UHealth and UnitedHealthcare in 2026

According to UnitedHealthcare’s own update (July 16, 2026), UHealth is currently out-of-network for every UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage plan except one: the Preferred Care Network plan. That one plan has a separate agreement — and UnitedHealthcare is ending it too, effective September 1, 2026, regardless of how the broader contract talks turn out.

Separately, UHealth and UnitedHealthcare are also negotiating a different contract covering Commercial, Exchange, and Medicaid plans, with its own July 31, 2026 deadline. That negotiation is not the same issue as the Medicare Advantage situation described here — don’t let the two get mixed up if you’re comparing information.

Which UnitedHealthcare Medicare Plans Are Affected — and When

  • Any UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage plan other than Preferred Care Network: UHealth is out-of-network now. If you’re not sure which plan you have, check your plan ID card or call the number on the back of it.
  • UnitedHealthcare Preferred Care Network (Medicare Advantage): Still in-network today. UHealth access ends September 1, 2026.
  • Emergency care at UHealth is always covered, regardless of network status, no matter which plan you have.

Does My Medicare Plan Still Cover Bascom Palmer or Sylvester?

If you’re on a UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage plan that isn’t Preferred Care Network, UHealth specialists — including Bascom Palmer Eye Institute and Sylvester Comprehensive Cancer Center — are already out-of-network. If you’re on Preferred Care Network specifically, you have access through August 31, 2026.

If you’re in active treatment for a serious condition (for example, ongoing chemotherapy or infusion therapy), you may qualify for continuity of care — a temporary extension of in-network benefits. UnitedHealthcare determines eligibility, not UHealth, so call the number on your plan ID card to ask directly.

Miami Medicare Members: What to Do Before September 1, 2026

A network change like this is exactly the kind of thing worth reviewing before you’re stuck making a decision under pressure. Depending on your situation, your options generally fall into a few categories:

  1. Check which specific plan you have. “UnitedHealthcare Medicare Advantage” covers several different plans in Miami-Dade and Broward, and only one of them (Preferred Care Network) still includes UHealth at all right now.
  2. Stay on your current plan and find in-network providers, if that works for your care.
  3. Review other Medicare Advantage or Medicare Supplement plans during this year’s Annual Enrollment Period (Oct. 15 – Dec. 7, 2026) to see which ones keep your current UHealth doctors in-network for 2027.
  4. Talk to your doctor’s office about which insurance plans they’re currently accepting, since that list is shifting as other carriers respond too.

None of these is automatically “right” — it depends on which doctors matter most to you, what your current plan costs, and what else is available in Miami-Dade and Broward for 2027. That’s the kind of comparison a licensed local agent can walk through with you at no cost.
