Unlocking the Secrets: Finding Preventative Services Under Medicare

Published June 8, 2025

One of Medicare's best-kept secrets is how much it covers for preventative care — often at absolutely no cost to you. Many Medicare beneficiaries skip important screenings and wellness visits simply because they don't know these services are covered. The result? Conditions that could have been caught early go undetected until they become serious — and expensive — health problems.

This guide walks you through every major preventative benefit available under Medicare Part B, so you can take full advantage of what you're already paying for.

First Things First: Your Welcome to Medicare Visit

When you first enroll in Medicare Part B, you're entitled to a one-time "Welcome to Medicare" preventive visit — also called the Initial Preventive Physical Examination (IPPE). This visit must take place within your first 12 months of having Medicare Part B coverage.

During this visit, your provider will:

  • Review your medical and family history
  • Take measurements (height, weight, blood pressure, vision)
  • Screen for potential risk factors and depression
  • Discuss advance care planning
  • Create a written plan to get you the preventive services you need

This visit is 100% covered by Medicare — no copay, no deductible — as long as your provider accepts Medicare assignment. Don't miss it. Think of it as your Medicare orientation checkup.

The Annual Wellness Visit (AWV): Not the Same as a Physical

After your first year of Medicare coverage, you become eligible for an Annual Wellness Visit (AWV) every 12 months. This is one of the most underused benefits in Medicare — and one of the most valuable.

Here's what many people don't realize: the Annual Wellness Visit is NOT a head-to-toe physical examination. It's a personalized prevention plan visit. Your doctor won't examine your body the way a traditional physical would. Instead, the AWV is focused on:

  • Updating your personal and family health history
  • Reviewing your current medications and supplements
  • Assessing your risk factors and cognitive function
  • Screening for depression and functional ability
  • Creating or updating a personalized prevention plan
  • Providing referrals for additional preventive services

The AWV is free under Medicare Part B — no copay, no cost-sharing — as long as you're seeing a Medicare-participating provider and the visit is billed correctly as a wellness visit. If your doctor performs additional diagnostic tests or addresses new health complaints during the same visit, those additional services may be billed separately and could carry a cost.

Cancer Screenings Covered by Medicare

Early detection saves lives. Medicare covers a robust set of cancer screenings at no cost to you:

Breast Cancer — Mammogram

Medicare covers one screening mammogram per year for women age 40 and older, at no cost. Women between 35 and 39 are also entitled to one baseline mammogram.

Colorectal Cancer — Colonoscopy & Other Screenings

Medicare covers screening colonoscopies once every 10 years (or every 2 years for those at high risk). Flexible sigmoidoscopies are covered every 48 months. Fecal occult blood tests and multi-target stool DNA tests are also covered. These screenings are free — no copay, no Part B deductible — when performed as a preventive screening.

Cervical & Vaginal Cancer — Pap Test

Medicare covers a Pap test and pelvic exam once every 24 months for most women, or annually for women at high risk for cervical or vaginal cancer.

Prostate Cancer

Medicare covers a PSA blood test and digital rectal exam once every 12 months for men age 50 and older. The PSA test has no cost-sharing under Medicare.

Lung Cancer — Low-Dose CT Scan

Medicare covers an annual low-dose CT lung cancer screening for adults aged 50–77 who are current smokers or have quit within the past 15 years, and who have a 20-pack-year smoking history. A written order from your doctor is required.

Other Essential Preventive Screenings

Cardiovascular Disease Screening

Medicare covers cardiovascular disease risk screenings — including cholesterol, lipid, and triglyceride blood tests — once every 5 years at no cost. Your doctor may also refer you to intensive behavioral therapy for cardiovascular disease if you have qualifying risk factors.

Diabetes Screening

If you have pre-diabetes or risk factors for diabetes (such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, obesity, or a family history of diabetes), Medicare covers up to two diabetes screening blood tests per year at no cost. This includes the fasting blood glucose test.

Bone Density — Osteoporosis Screening

Medicare covers bone mass measurement tests every 24 months — or more frequently if medically necessary — for those at risk for osteoporosis. This is particularly important for women over 65 and anyone on long-term steroid medications.

Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm Screening

If you are a man between 65 and 75 who has smoked at least 100 cigarettes in your lifetime, Medicare covers a one-time ultrasound screening for abdominal aortic aneurysm at no cost.

Depression Screening

Medicare covers one annual depression screening in a primary care setting at no cost. Early identification of depression leads to better outcomes and is a critical part of whole-person wellness.

Vaccines Covered Under Medicare

Vaccinations are among the simplest ways to prevent serious illness, and Medicare Part B covers several important vaccines at no cost:

  • Flu vaccine — One shot per flu season, every year
  • Pneumococcal vaccines — Two shots covering different strains of pneumonia (PCV15/PCV20 and PPSV23), typically given at least one year apart
  • COVID-19 vaccine — Covered under Part B, including updated boosters
  • Hepatitis B vaccine — Covered for adults at medium or high risk

Note: Shingles (Shingrix) and some other vaccines are typically covered under Medicare Part D (prescription drug coverage), not Part B. Check your specific plan for details.

The Medicare Diabetes Prevention Program (MDPP)

If you have pre-diabetes, Medicare covers a structured Diabetes Prevention Program (MDPP) at no cost. This CDC-recognized program helps people with pre-diabetes make lifestyle changes — diet, physical activity, and behavioral support — to prevent or delay the onset of Type 2 diabetes. It is offered by approved suppliers in person or virtually and typically spans 12 months.

Important Rules: How to Ensure No-Cost Preventive Care

To receive preventive services with no out-of-pocket costs, keep these key rules in mind:

  • Use a Medicare-participating provider. Your doctor or specialist must accept Medicare assignment. Providers who don't accept Medicare assignment can charge more.
  • Make sure the visit is billed as preventive. If your provider addresses a new or existing medical problem during the same visit, those services may be billed separately — and your deductible or copay could apply.
  • Know your eligibility windows. Many screenings have specific time intervals (annually, every 2 years, every 10 years). Scheduling a screening before the allowed interval could result in cost-sharing.
  • Ask before your visit. Always confirm with your provider's office that the service will be billed as a preventive screening under Medicare guidelines.
