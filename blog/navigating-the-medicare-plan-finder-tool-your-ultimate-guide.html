Navigating the Medicare Plan Finder Tool: Your Ultimate Guide

Published April 20, 2025

If you've ever felt overwhelmed trying to compare Medicare plans, you're not alone. With dozens of Medicare Advantage and Part D prescription drug plans available in Florida, finding the right coverage can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack. The good news? The federal government provides a powerful — and free — resource to help: the Medicare Plan Finder at Medicare.gov/plan-compare. This step-by-step guide will walk you through exactly how to use it.

What Is the Medicare Plan Finder?

The Medicare Plan Finder is an official tool on Medicare.gov that allows you to search, filter, and compare Medicare health and drug plans available in your area. Whether you're looking at Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans that bundle hospital, medical, and often drug coverage into one plan, or standalone Part D prescription drug plans to complement your Original Medicare, the Plan Finder puts all your options side by side in one place — completely free of charge.

It's updated annually with plan data for the upcoming plan year, making it the go-to resource during the Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) and whenever you're first enrolling in Medicare.

What You Need Before You Start

To get the most personalized and accurate results, have the following information ready before you log in:

  • Your Medicare Number — the red-white-and-blue Medicare card has an 11-character Medicare Beneficiary Identifier (MBI).
  • Your Part A and Part B effective dates — these are printed on the front of your Medicare card and confirm when your coverage began.
  • Your ZIP code — plan availability varies by county and ZIP code, especially in Florida where coverage can differ between Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties.
  • A list of your current prescription drugs — include the drug name, dosage, and how often you take it. This is crucial for calculating accurate drug costs.
  • Your preferred doctors and specialists — if staying with your current physicians matters to you, you'll want to check each plan's network.

You can use the tool as a guest without logging in, but signing in with your Medicare.gov account gives you access to pre-loaded drug lists and more personalized cost estimates.

Step-by-Step: How to Use the Medicare Plan Finder

Step 1 — Go to Medicare.gov/plan-compare

Open your browser and navigate to Medicare.gov/plan-compare. You'll see options to search as a guest or log in with your Medicare.gov credentials. Logging in allows the tool to pull in your existing drug information if you've entered it before.

Step 2 — Enter Your ZIP Code

Start by entering your ZIP code. The tool will display plans available specifically in your area. In South Florida, the number of available plans can be quite large — this is why filtering is so important.

Step 3 — Select the Type of Coverage You Want

Choose between:

  • Medicare Advantage Plans (Part C) — these replace Original Medicare and often include prescription drug coverage, dental, vision, hearing, and fitness benefits.
  • Medicare Prescription Drug Plans (Part D) — standalone drug plans that work alongside Original Medicare (Parts A and B) or a Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plan.
  • Medicare Advantage Plans with Drug Coverage (MAPD) — the most common choice; an all-in-one plan.

Step 4 — Add Your Prescription Drugs

This step is one of the most important. Click Add Drugs and enter each medication you take. The tool will then calculate your estimated annual drug costs for each plan, factoring in copays, deductibles, and the coverage gap. Skipping this step means you won't see accurate cost comparisons — two plans with the same premium can have dramatically different out-of-pocket drug costs.

Step 5 — Add Your Preferred Doctors

Use the Add Doctors feature to search for your primary care physician and any specialists you see regularly. The tool will flag whether each doctor is in-network for the plans you're comparing. Out-of-network care can be significantly more expensive — or not covered at all — on HMO-style plans.

Step 6 — Review and Filter Your Results

The Plan Finder will now display all available plans matching your criteria. You can filter by:

  • Monthly premium range
  • Plan type (HMO, PPO, PFFS, SNP)
  • Star rating (quality rating)
  • Drug coverage
  • Extra benefits (dental, vision, hearing, transportation)

Use the Compare checkbox to select up to three plans at a time and view them in a side-by-side comparison that shows premiums, deductibles, copays, and estimated yearly costs.

Understanding Medicare Star Ratings

Every Medicare Advantage and Part D plan receives an annual quality rating from the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS). Ratings run on a scale of 1 to 5 stars, where:

  • ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5 Stars — Excellent quality; you can enroll in a 5-star plan any time of year using a special enrollment period.
  • ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4 Stars — Above average; a solid indicator of quality and member satisfaction.
  • ⭐⭐⭐ 3 Stars — Average performance.
  • ⭐⭐ 2 Stars or below — Below average; Medicare monitors these plans closely.

As a general rule of thumb, 4 stars or above is considered a good benchmark when evaluating plan quality. Star ratings factor in things like how well the plan manages chronic conditions, member complaints, customer service, and how quickly members can get appointments.

Key Dates to Keep in Mind

The Medicare Plan Finder is especially important to use during two critical windows each year:

  • Annual Notice of Change (ANOC) — September: Every September, your current plan mails you an ANOC letter detailing any changes to premiums, benefits, or drug coverage for the coming year. This is your cue to log into the Plan Finder and check whether your plan is still the best fit.
  • Annual Enrollment Period (AEP) — October 15 to December 7: This is the main window when you can switch Medicare Advantage plans, switch from Original Medicare to Medicare Advantage (or vice versa), or change your Part D drug plan. Changes take effect January 1 of the following year.

Don't wait until December to start comparing. Plans can fill up, and you want time to review your options carefully.

Tips for Florida Beneficiaries

Florida — and South Florida in particular — has one of the most competitive Medicare markets in the country. That means more plan choices, but also more complexity. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Plan networks in Miami-Dade and Broward can differ significantly even within the same insurance carrier. Always verify your doctors before enrolling.
  • Many Florida Medicare Advantage plans include extra benefits like over-the-counter allowances, transportation, and meal delivery. Factor these into your total value calculation.
  • If you travel frequently between Florida and another state, a PPO plan or Original Medicare with a Medigap policy may give you more flexibility than an HMO.
