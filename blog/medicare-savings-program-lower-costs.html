How the Medicare Savings Program Can Lower Your Costs

The Health Experts Insurance · Saving Money on Medicare

Here's a number that surprises a lot of our clients: the standard Medicare Part B premium is deducted from your Social Security check every single month — and for many people on a fixed income, that's real money. What surprises them even more? There's a program that may pay that premium for you, and thousands of eligible South Floridians never apply. It's called the Medicare Savings Program (MSP), and it's one of the most under-used benefits we see.

What the Medicare Savings Program actually does

The MSP is a set of programs run by each state (in Florida, through the Department of Children and Families) that help people with limited income and resources pay their Medicare costs. Depending on which level you qualify for, the program can:

  • Pay your monthly Part B premium — putting that money back in your Social Security check
  • Pay your Part A premium, if you have one
  • Cover Medicare deductibles, coinsurance, and copays (at the QMB level)

The three main levels

ProgramWhat it helps pay
QMB (Qualified Medicare Beneficiary)Part A and B premiums, plus deductibles, coinsurance, and copays
SLMB (Specified Low-Income Medicare Beneficiary)Part B premium
QI (Qualifying Individual)Part B premium, at a slightly higher income limit than SLMB

Income and resource limits are set by the federal government and adjust every year, so instead of quoting numbers that may be outdated by the time you read this, here's our honest advice: if money is tight, get screened. The check takes minutes, it's free, and there's no penalty for applying and not qualifying.

The bonus most people don't know about: Extra Help

Qualifying for an MSP automatically qualifies you for Extra Help (also called the Low-Income Subsidy), which lowers your Part D prescription drug costs — premiums and copays. For someone taking several medications, this can be one of the most meaningful benefits of the whole program.

MSP eligibility can also open a Special Enrollment Period, meaning you may be able to review your coverage options outside the normal enrollment windows. And if you qualify for both Medicare and Medicaid, there are plan types designed specifically for dual-eligible beneficiaries — you can learn more about how coverage pieces fit together on our Medicare FAQ page.

How to apply in Florida

Applications go through Florida's Department of Children and Families (DCF). You'll typically need documentation of your income (like Social Security award letters), bank statements, and identification. You can apply on your own — or you can let a licensed agent walk you through it at no cost. Frequently asked questions

What is the Medicare Savings Program?

A set of state-administered programs that help people with limited income and resources pay Medicare costs — including the Part B premium and, at some levels, deductibles, coinsurance, and copays.

What are the levels of the Medicare Savings Program?

QMB (pays Part A and B premiums plus cost-sharing), SLMB (pays the Part B premium), and QI (pays the Part B premium at a slightly higher income limit).

Does the Medicare Savings Program help with prescription drug costs?

Yes — qualifying for an MSP automatically qualifies you for Extra Help, which lowers Part D premiums and copays.

How do I apply for the Medicare Savings Program in Florida?

Through the Department of Children and Families (DCF). A licensed agent or community partner can help you gather documents and apply at no cost.
