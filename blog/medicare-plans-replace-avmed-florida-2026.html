Medicare Plans That Replaced AvMed in Florida 2026

Published May 12, 2026

When AvMed announced it was ending its Medicare Advantage program in Florida, the question every member asked us was the same: "So what do I do now?"

The longer version of that question is usually: "What plans are available where I live? Which ones cover my doctor? Which ones cover my prescriptions without me paying a fortune? Are any of them as good as what I had?"

We can't answer those questions for everyone in a blog post — every situation is different and Medicare plans vary by your specific ZIP code, doctors, and medications. But what we can do is give you a clear, honest map of the Medicare Advantage landscape in South Florida after AvMed's exit, so you know what carriers are operating here and what to look at when you compare.

This post is the "what's out there" companion to our What to Do Now post. If you haven't read that one yet, start there.

A Few Things We Won't Do in This Post

We will not rank these carriers. We will not recommend specific plans. We will not exaggerate benefits.

Why? Because the "best" carrier depends entirely on your doctors, your prescriptions, and your health needs. What works for your neighbor might not work for you.

What we will do is give you a straightforward look at who's here, what they offer, and what to check before you enroll.

The Major Medicare Advantage Carriers in South Florida

We've spent the last several months sitting across the table from former AvMed members. Here's who's serving South Florida and what you should know about each.

Humana

Network presence: Strong across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach. One of the largest Medicare Advantage carriers in Florida.

Plan types: HMO, PPO, and HMO-POS plans. Wide range of $0-premium options.

Notable benefits: Healthy Options allowance (OTC card), dental, vision, hearing, SilverSneakers fitness, telehealth, hearing aids.

What makes them different: Humana has a lot of provider relationships in South Florida, which means decent network depth. They also own CarePlus (see below).

UnitedHealthcare (UHC)

Network presence: Very strong. UHC is one of the largest health insurers in the country and has deep roots in Florida.

Plan types: HMO, PPO, and Dual Special Needs Plans (D-SNP for Medicaid-eligible members).

Notable benefits: OTC card, dental, vision, hearing, fitness (Renew Active program), telehealth, transportation to appointments.

What makes them different: UHC's network tends to be large, which gives you more doctor and specialist options. They also own Preferred Care Partners (PCP), a South Florida-focused brand.

Aetna

Network presence: Solid in Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach.

Plan types: HMO and PPO plans.

Notable benefits: Healthy Foods card (OTC plus groceries), dental, vision, hearing, fitness, telehealth.

What makes them different: Aetna's Healthy Foods benefit is broader than typical OTC cards — you can buy fresh produce and healthy groceries, not just vitamins and band-aids.

Cigna HealthSpring

Network presence: Well-established in South Florida, especially Miami-Dade.

Plan types: HMO plans, many with $0 premiums.

Notable benefits: OTC allowance, dental, vision, hearing, fitness, telehealth.

What makes them different: HealthSpring has been in the South Florida Medicare market for years. They tend to have stable networks and straightforward plan designs.

Devoted Health

Network presence: Growing quickly in South Florida. Newer carrier, but expanding fast.

Plan types: PPO plans (no referrals required).

Notable benefits: Dental, vision, hearing, fitness, OTC allowance, care navigation team (your own support team), transportation.

What makes them different: Devoted is a tech-forward carrier. Members get a dedicated care team, a mobile app, and a lot of hand-holding. If you want a more personalized experience, they're worth looking at.

WellCare (now part of Centene)

Network presence: Strong across Florida, including South Florida.

Plan types: HMO and D-SNP plans.

Notable benefits: OTC card, dental, vision, hearing, fitness, meal delivery after hospital stays.

What makes them different: WellCare has been serving Florida Medicaid and Medicare for decades. They have a lot of experience with lower-income and dual-eligible members.

CarePlus (Humana Subsidiary)

Network presence: Miami-Dade and Broward focused. Very local.

Plan types: HMO plans.

Notable benefits: Similar to Humana (OTC, dental, vision, fitness, telehealth).

What makes them different: CarePlus is Humana's South Florida-specific brand. If you see a CarePlus plan, it's Humana under a different name with a more local network focus.

Doctors HealthCare Plans (DHCP)

Network presence: Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach. South Florida-only carrier.

Plan types: HMO plans.

Notable benefits: OTC allowance, dental, vision, hearing, fitness.

What makes them different: DHCP is a local carrier — they only operate in South Florida. That can mean tighter local provider relationships, but it also means limited coverage if you travel outside Florida frequently.

Preferred Care Partners (PCP, UnitedHealthcare Subsidiary)

Network presence: Miami-Dade and Broward. Local brand.

Plan types: HMO plans.

Notable benefits: Similar to UHC (OTC, dental, vision, fitness, transportation).

What makes them different: Like CarePlus, PCP is a localized brand (owned by UHC) with South Florida-focused networks.

HealthSun

Network presence: Growing in South Florida.

Plan types: HMO plans.

Notable benefits: OTC allowance, dental, vision, hearing, fitness.

What makes them different: HealthSun has been expanding in Florida over the last few years. Smaller carrier, but they're active in the market.

Simply Healthcare (Anthem Subsidiary)

Network presence: Statewide, including South Florida.

Plan types: HMO and D-SNP plans.

Notable benefits: OTC card, dental, vision, hearing, fitness, telehealth.

What makes them different: Simply serves both commercial and Medicare members in Florida. They've been in the state for years and have a broad network.

How to Actually Compare Them

Knowing who's out there is step one. Step two is comparing them in a way that actually matters to your situation.

Here are the four things you should check before you enroll in any Medicare Advantage plan:

1. Are Your Doctors In-Network?

This is non-negotiable. If your primary care doctor or specialists aren't in the plan's network, you'll either pay out-of-pocket (if it's a PPO) or you won't be covered at all (if it's an HMO).

Don't assume. Call your doctor's office and ask: "Do you accept [carrier name] Medicare Advantage?" Or ask us — we check this for you before enrollment.

2. Are Your Prescriptions on the Formulary at What Cost Tier?

Every Medicare Advantage plan has a formulary (the list of covered drugs). Your medication might be on the list, but it matters which tier it's on.

Tier 1 or 2? Low copay. Tier 4 or 5? You might pay hundreds per month.

We run your medication list through every carrier's formulary and show you the exact cost difference.

3. What's the Total Cost Picture?

Don't just look at the monthly premium. Look at:

  • Monthly premium ($0 to $100+)
  • Copays for doctor visits (primary care and specialists)
  • Copays for prescriptions
  • Copays for hospital stays, outpatient surgery, imaging
  • Annual maximum out-of-pocket limit

A $0 premium plan can cost you more than a $50/month plan if you see specialists regularly or take expensive medications.

4. Do the Extra Benefits Actually Matter to You?

OTC cards, gym memberships, dental, vision, hearing — these are all nice. But if you don't use them, they don't add value.

Focus on the benefits that you will actually use. If you already have dental insurance or don't go to the gym, those perks don't matter.

FAQs

Can I switch from AvMed to a new carrier anytime?

If you're still in your Initial Enrollment Period or a Special Enrollment Period, yes. Outside of those windows, you'll need to wait for the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15–December 7) or the Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (January 1–March 31).

Do all these carriers cover the same doctors?

No. Every carrier has its own network. That's why you need to verify your doctors are in-network before you enroll.

Which carrier is best?

There's no universal "best" carrier. The best plan for you depends on your doctors, prescriptions, and health needs. That's why we compare them side by side for each person.

Can I go back to Original Medicare if I don't like Medicare Advantage?

Yes, but you may have trouble getting a Medigap (Medicare Supplement) plan without medical underwriting. If you're within 12 months of enrolling in Medicare Part B for the first time, you have guaranteed-issue rights. After that, insurers can deny you or charge more based on health conditions.
