AvMed Medicare Ended in Florida: What to Do Now

Published May 12, 2026

If you were enrolled in an AvMed Medicare Advantage plan in Florida, you've already lived through one of the most significant Medicare disruptions in recent memory. AvMed's Medicare Advantage plans ended on December 31, 2025, leaving thousands of Florida seniors without the coverage they counted on. Whether you found a new plan during open enrollment or are still sorting out what happened, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know — and what you can do right now.

What Happened with AvMed Medicare?

AvMed, a long-standing Florida health insurer, announced in 2025 that it would exit the Medicare Advantage market at the end of the year. Effective January 1, 2026, all AvMed Medicare Advantage plans were discontinued statewide. This type of exit is called a plan non-renewal, and it is governed by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS).

When a Medicare Advantage plan exits the market, CMS requires the insurer to notify all affected members in writing well in advance. Those notices explained that coverage would end December 31, 2025, and that members needed to take action to avoid a gap in benefits.

What Should Have Happened — and What Still Can

The Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

The primary window for AvMed members to find new coverage was the Annual Enrollment Period (AEP), which ran from October 15 to December 7, 2025. During AEP, all Medicare beneficiaries — including AvMed members — could compare plans and enroll in new Medicare Advantage or Part D prescription drug coverage effective January 1, 2026.

If You Didn't Switch — What Happened to Your Coverage?

If you were an AvMed Medicare Advantage member and did not select a new plan before December 7, 2025, here is what CMS rules say happened:

  • You were automatically disenrolled from your AvMed plan on December 31, 2025.
  • You were returned to Original Medicare (Parts A and B) only, effective January 1, 2026.
  • You lost the extra benefits your AvMed plan provided — including dental, vision, hearing, and prescription drug (Part D) coverage.
  • A Special Enrollment Period (SEP) was triggered by the plan exit, giving you additional time to enroll in a new plan.

Do You Still Have Time to Enroll in a New Plan?

Possibly — and this is important. When a Medicare Advantage plan exits the market, affected members typically receive a Special Enrollment Period that allows them to join a new plan outside of the regular AEP window. This SEP window is generally available for a limited time after the plan's termination date, so acting quickly matters.

If you are currently on Original Medicare without drug coverage, there may also be other qualifying events or enrollment windows that apply to your specific situation.

The most important thing: don't wait. Every month without proper prescription or medical coverage is a month of potential out-of-pocket exposure. A licensed Medicare broker can review your situation at no cost and tell you exactly what options are open to you right now.

What Plans Are Available in Florida?

Florida is one of the most competitive Medicare Advantage markets in the entire country — which is actually good news for former AvMed members. There are numerous plans available from major carriers throughout South Florida and the state, each with different networks, benefits, and costs.

Some of the insurers active in Florida's Medicare Advantage market include national and regional carriers such as Humana, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Devoted Health, and CarePlus, among others. The right plan for you depends on your health needs, your doctors, your prescriptions, and your budget — not on a general ranking.

When comparing plans, focus on these key factors:

  • Your doctors and specialists — Are they in the plan's provider network?
  • Your prescriptions — Are your medications covered, and at which cost tier?
  • Extra benefits — Dental, vision, hearing, over-the-counter allowances, transportation, and fitness programs vary widely by plan.
  • Your costs — Monthly premiums, copays, deductibles, and annual out-of-pocket maximums differ significantly.
  • CMS Star Ratings — Plans are rated 1–5 stars by CMS based on quality and member satisfaction.

As independent brokers, we work with multiple carriers — so we compare your options side by side and help you find the plan that fits, not just the plan that's available.

What About Your Doctors?

This is the number-one question we hear: "Will I keep my doctors?" When you transition from AvMed to a different Medicare Advantage plan, there is no guarantee that your current physicians will be in-network with your new plan. Every plan has its own provider network, and networks can differ dramatically even within the same county.

This is why working with a broker matters. Before you enroll in anything, we verify your specific doctors, specialists, and preferred pharmacy in each plan's directory. We do this work for you — at no cost.

Steps to Protect Your Doctor Relationships

  • Ask your doctor's office which Medicare Advantage plans they currently accept.
  • Check the plan's online provider directory before enrolling.
  • Confirm your pharmacy is in-network or that mail-order options are available.
  • Make sure your current prescriptions are on the plan's formulary at an affordable tier.
