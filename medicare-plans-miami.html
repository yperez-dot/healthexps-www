Medicare plan options in South Florida

There are three main ways to get your Medicare coverage. Our brokers explain all of them and help you decide which is right for your situation.

Medicare Advantage (Part C)

An all-in-one alternative to Original Medicare offered by private insurers. Usually includes dental, vision, hearing, and drug coverage — often with a $0 premium.

  • Many $0 premium plans in Miami-Dade
  • Annual out-of-pocket maximum protects you
  • Dental, vision, hearing often included
  • OTC allowance, gym membership, transportation
  • Network-based — verify your doctors first
Learn about Medicare Advantage →
Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Works alongside Original Medicare to fill the 20% coverage gap. See any doctor in the country that accepts Medicare — no networks, no referrals.

  • Nationwide coverage — no network restrictions
  • Predictable costs — no surprise bills
  • Plan G covers nearly everything except Part B deductible
  • Premium varies by age and carrier — compare prices
  • Add a separate Part D drug plan
Learn about Medicare Supplement →
Medicare Part D (Drug Plans)

Prescription drug coverage added to Original Medicare or a standalone Medigap plan. Required to avoid late enrollment penalties if you have prescriptions.

  • $2,000 out-of-pocket cap in 2026
  • Formularies vary — match to your medications
  • Premium, deductible, and copays vary by plan
  • Extra Help available for limited income
  • Review annually during AEP (Oct 15–Dec 7)

COMMON MISTAKES

Medicare myths that cost Floridians thousands

One wrong assumption can mean permanent penalties or years of overpaying.

MYTH
"I have to be retired to get Medicare."
FALSE — you qualify at 65 regardless of work status.
MYTH
"COBRA extends my Medicare window."
FALSE — your SEP clock starts when employer coverage ends.
MYTH
"A $0 plan is always the best deal."
FALSE — out-of-pocket costs can reach $9,350/year.
