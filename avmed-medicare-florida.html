What happened to your AvMed Medicare coverage?

Here's exactly what happened and what your current status is.

📋
Your plan was terminated

AvMed sent notices to all Medicare members informing them that Medicare Advantage and Medicare Supplement plans would not be renewed for 2026. Effective December 31, 2025, all AvMed Medicare coverage ended.

🏛️
You're now on Original Medicare

If you did not enroll in a new plan before January 1, 2026, you were automatically returned to Original Medicare (Parts A and B). This means you have hospital and medical coverage — but no drug coverage, no dental or vision, and no out-of-pocket cap.

⏰
You have a Special Enrollment Period

Because your plan was involuntarily terminated, you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period (SEP) to enroll in a new Medicare Advantage or Medicare Supplement plan. You don't have to wait for the Annual Enrollment Period. Act now — don't go without coverage.

💊
Your drug coverage may have lapsed

If your AvMed plan included prescription drug coverage (MAPD), you no longer have Part D coverage either. If you take medications, enrolling in a new plan with drug coverage — or adding a standalone Part D plan — is urgent.

Your replacement plan options

Three solid paths forward — depending on your doctors, budget, and preferences.

⭐
Medicare Advantage (Part C)

An all-in-one replacement that bundles Part A, B, and usually Part D. Often includes dental, vision, and hearing. Many plans in South Florida have $0 premiums. Network-based — we'll verify your doctors are covered.

🛡️
Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Works alongside Original Medicare to fill the 20% gap. No networks — see any doctor that accepts Medicare. Plan G covers almost everything. Add a Part D plan for drugs. Best for those who want maximum freedom.

Original Medicare + Part D

If you're not ready to add a full plan, at minimum add a Part D drug plan to cover your prescriptions. Original Medicare alone has no drug coverage or OOP cap — we recommend adding at least Part D immediately.
